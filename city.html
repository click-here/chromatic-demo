How the city works

Your real Claude Code sessions feed the city. A burst is a stretch of real work in a session (tool calls and turns since the last check) · not elapsed time.

The city follows one master plan. Your bursts raise it stage by stage · nothing to buy, nothing to place. Building rises on real work, never by the clock alone. Paused work just waits · points never revert.

Brick pays on every burst and measures steady work.
Glass pays only when 2+ sessions work at once.
Gold leaf pays two ways: rescue a session that sat idle 5+ minutes (+1 to +3 by idle time), or set a personal best day (+2).

Materials are never spent. They are meters of how you work, and they open views: parallel work opens the overlook and the rooftop · rescues and record days open the train line and the cinematic tour. A view, once open, stays open forever.

While you are away the city banks a quiet trickle: 0.1 brick per minute, capped at 100 per day, outside the haul.

Every finished building keeps a plaque of who raised it. Nothing decays · nothing is ever taken away. The bottom caption shows the newest payout · the event log layer keeps the last few on screen · click a building for its plaque.
